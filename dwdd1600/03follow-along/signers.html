Several signers of the Declaration of Independence became especially famous because of their roles in the American Revolution and the founding of the United States:

John Hancock
President of the Second Continental Congress. Famous for his large, bold signature, which became synonymous with "signature" itself.
Thomas Jefferson
Primary author of the Declaration of Independence. Later became the third President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Renowned inventor, scientist, writer, and diplomat. Helped secure French support during the Revolutionary War.
John Adams
Strong advocate for independence. Later became the second President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
Prominent revolutionary and organizer of resistance to British rule. Associated with the movement that led to the Boston Tea Party.
Roger Sherman
The only person to sign the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, the United States Constitution, and the Continental Association.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Catholic signer of the Declaration of Independence and the last surviving signer, living until 1832.
Richard Henry Lee
Introduced the resolution in Congress declaring the colonies independent from Great Britain.
Benjamin Rush
Influential physician, educator, reformer, and Founding Father.
Elbridge Gerry
Signer of the Declaration who later served as Vice President of the United States. The term "gerrymandering" is derived from his name.
John Adams
Massachusetts lawyer, advocate for independence, and member of the committee that drafted the Declaration. Later became the second President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
Massachusetts revolutionary leader who helped organize colonial resistance to British rule and promoted the cause of independence.
Josiah Bartlett
New Hampshire physician and statesman who was among the first delegates to vote for independence.

Charles Carroll of Carrollton

Abraham Clark
New Jersey surveyor, lawyer, and patriot who represented his state in the Continental Congress.
George Clymer
